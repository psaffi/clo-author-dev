% Information Acquisition Channel

The baseline results establish that active funds reduce positions in stocks they lend. We now investigate the mechanism: do funds trade on information acquired through the lending process? We exploit mandatory short position disclosure events as an exogenous information shock to identify the information acquisition channel.

\subsection{Mandatory Short Position Disclosure}

Under EU regulation, investors must publicly disclose net short positions exceeding 0.5\% of a company's issued share capital. We construct an indicator variable $Disclosure_{i,t+1}$ equal to one if stock $i$ is subject to a mandatory short position disclosure in month $t+1$. If lending funds extract information from borrowing demand, the trading response should be stronger when the lending activity is associated with a subsequent disclosure event---precisely the cases where short sellers are building large, informed positions.

Table~\ref{table_disclosure} presents the results. Column~(1) reproduces the baseline for the active fund sample ($OnLoan_{i,j,t-1} = -0.2837$, $t = -2.85$). Column~(2) adds the disclosure indicator, which is positive but insignificant ($0.3633$, $t = 1.25$). Column~(3) introduces the key interaction: $OnLoan_{i,j,t-1} \times Disclosure_{i,t+1}$. The interaction coefficient is $-0.8027$ and statistically significant at the 5\% level ($t = -2.68$). Active funds reduce their positions substantially more when lending precedes a disclosure event. Column~(4) adds the full set of saturated fixed effects (security$\times$time, fund$\times$time, fund$\times$security); the interaction strengthens to $-0.8622$ ($t = -2.22$).

Column~(5) provides the critical placebo test. We estimate the identical specification for passive funds only. The coefficient on $OnLoan_{i,j,t-1}$ is economically small and statistically insignificant ($0.0208$, $t = 0.23$), and the interaction with $Disclosure_{i,t+1}$ is precisely zero ($0.0258$, $t = 0.28$). Passive funds---which cannot adjust their portfolios in response to lending information---show no trading reaction. This placebo result strongly supports the information acquisition interpretation over mechanical alternatives.

\subsection{Passive Fund Lending as Alternative Measure}

A potential concern is that a fund's own lending activity is endogenous to its trading decisions. To address this, we replace $OnLoan_{i,j,t-1}$ with $OnLoan_{i,f\text{-}passive,t-1}$, which measures lending by passive funds in the same family. Because passive funds cannot trade on information, their lending is driven by portfolio composition and borrower demand rather than by strategic considerations.

Table~\ref{table_passive_iv} presents these results. The coefficient on passive fund lending is $-0.3406$ ($t = -3.20$) in column~(1), comparable to the baseline specification. The interaction with $Disclosure_{i,t+1}$ in column~(3) is $-1.1240$ and significant at the 1\% level ($t = -3.29$)---larger in magnitude than the baseline interaction, consistent with passive fund lending providing a cleaner measure of exogenous borrowing demand. Column~(4) confirms robustness with saturated fixed effects ($-0.9156$, $t = -2.32$).

\subsection{Timing Tests: Leads and Lags of Disclosure}

If active funds genuinely acquire information through lending and trade in anticipation of future short-selling pressure, only the \textit{lead} disclosure should predict their trading. Contemporaneous and lagged disclosures should be uninformative, as the information acquisition occurs before the disclosure event.

Table~\ref{table_timing} tests this prediction by including leads, contemporaneous, and lagged disclosure indicators along with their interactions with $OnLoan$. Column~(3) shows that only the lead interaction ($OnLoan \times Disclosure_{i,t+1}$) is statistically significant ($-0.8224$, $t = -2.62$). The contemporaneous interaction is economically negligible ($0.0353$, $t = 0.08$) and the lagged interaction is small and insignificant ($-0.1157$, $t = -0.62$). Column~(4) with saturated fixed effects confirms this pattern: the lead interaction is $-0.8723$ ($t = -2.27$), while contemporaneous and lagged interactions remain insignificant.

This timing pattern is precisely what the information acquisition channel predicts. Funds trade in advance of disclosure events---not in response to them---consistent with observing borrowing demand before it becomes public knowledge.
