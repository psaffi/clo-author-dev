{\footnotesize
\textit{Notes:} The table reports results from panel regressions of $PosChange_{i,j,t}$, the monthly percentage change in fund $j$'s position in stock $i$, on family-level securities lending interacted with measures of lending market concentration. The sample is restricted to actively managed funds in the disclosure sample (within $\pm$3 months of a first-time large short position disclosure) for stocks that were ever publicly disclosed. $\mathit{OnLoan}_{i,f,t-1}$ is the aggregate dollar value of stock $i$ lent by family $f$ in month $t-1$ divided by the market capitalization of stock $i$, standardized to mean zero and unit standard deviation. $\mathit{Disclosure}_{i,t+1}$ equals one if stock $i$ has a new public short position disclosure in month $t+1$ (i.e., no prior disclosure in the preceding six months). All concentration measures are standardized: $\mathit{HHI}^{\mathit{stock}}_{i,t-1}$ is the Herfindahl--Hirschman index of lending concentration across families for stock $i$; $1/\mathit{ActiveAgents}_{i,t-1}$ is the inverse of the number of active lending agents for stock $i$; $\mathit{HHI}^{\mathit{family}}_{f,t-1}$ is the HHI of family $f$'s lending portfolio across stocks; $1/\mathit{StocksHeld}_{f,t-1}$ is the inverse of the number of stocks held by family $f$ ($\times 10^4$); $1/\mathit{StocksLent}_{f,t-1}$ is the inverse of the number of stocks lent by family $f$ ($\times 10^4$). Controls include lagged Amihud illiquidity rank and lagged position weight. All specifications include security$\times$time, fund$\times$time, and fund$\times$security fixed effects, with standard errors double-clustered by fund and time. \textit{t}-statistics in parentheses. *, **, *** denote significance at the 10\%, 5\%, and 1\% levels.
